2. Program Context

  • PathEDU is an Intuit program that trains and hires university students to handle customer calls — a different pipeline from the traditional "street to seat" hiring channel.
  • Two roles: Tax Specialists (TS) handle tax-related customer inquiries; Product Support (PS) handles product/technical issues.
  • Two partners participating this year:
    • Education at Work — exclusively works with PathEDU (both TS and PS)
    • Foundever — only Marshall University participates in PathEDU; Foundever also has many other non-PathEDU workstreams
  • NonPathEDU comparison: For TS, the comparison is against Intuit-sourced experts. For PS, NonPathEDU includes multiple partners (Teleperformance, Concentrix, LiveOps, Working Solutions, and Foundever's non-PathEDU workstreams).

SKU Mix Finding — A Counterintuitive Result:
  • PathEDU handles significantly simpler cases. 53% Basic vs 41% NonPathEDU, and only 10% Premium vs 24% NonPathEDU. With simpler cases, we would expect lower CST — but PathEDU's CST is higher (5.06 vs 4.24).
  • This means the true within-SKU CST gap is likely even larger than the headline number. If PathEDU handled the same product mix as NonPathEDU (i.e., more Premium), their CST would be even higher. The simpler mix is actually masking a bigger efficiency gap.
  • Conversion rate gap is partially explained by mix: Basic returns may have inherently lower conversion potential, and PathEDU's heavier Basic mix (53% vs 41%) could contribute to the 2.5pp conversion deficit.
  • tNPS advantage is likely genuine: PathEDU's higher tNPS (83.24 vs 79.55) exists despite handling simpler cases, which may have less room for expert value-add. This suggests PathEDU students genuinely deliver better customer experiences.
  • Foundever's pattern confirms the relationship: 64% Basic mix and CST of 4.06 — the simplest mix produces the lowest CST, as expected. EAW's CST (5.14) remains elevated despite a 52% Basic mix, reinforcing the efficiency concern.
Implication for CST: The SKU mix analysis does not explain PathEDU's elevated CST — it actually makes the gap more concerning. PathEDU experts are spending more time per engagement on simpler cases. This strongly suggests a behavioral/training gap rather than a case complexity driver. Targeted efficiency coaching (call pacing, time management, structured wrap-up) should be a priority.

6. Conclusions & Key Takeaways

Tax Specialist Findings

  1. PathEDU delivers higher customer satisfaction. FS tNPS is 3.1 points higher (82.59 vs 79.48) across all segments. Re-Hires achieve an exceptional 89.83 tNPS.
  2. CST and AHT are significantly elevated — an efficiency concern compounded by SKU mix. PathEDU experts spend more time per engagement (CST 5.16 vs 4.22) and have higher AHT (28.15 vs 25.54 min). Critically, SKU mix analysis reveals PathEDU handles simpler cases (53% Basic vs 41%, only 10% Premium vs 24%), meaning the true within-SKU CST gap is even larger. This points to a behavioral/training gap, not case complexity.
  3. Conversion rate needs attention. PathEDU lags by ~3.6 pp overall (59.49% vs 63.10%), with Re-Hires showing a wider gap (−7.04 pp). Targeted conversion training could close this gap.
  4. Education at Work is the strong TS partner. Consistent 82+ tNPS for New Hires, 88 for Re-Hires, and reasonable volume. Foundever's TS program is too small for reliable conclusions.
  5. EAW Wave 3 has the best balance (tNPS 83.81, Conv 62.22%, lowest CST 4.79). Later waves show worsening CST without proportional satisfaction gains — efficiency coaching is needed.

Product Support Findings

  1. PathEDU outperforms on both key metrics. AHT is 10% faster (17.53 vs 19.58 min) and tNPS is 3.0 points higher (48.04 vs 45.07). This is a strong result — faster and better-rated.
  2. The advantage holds for both New Hires and Re-Hires. New Hire tNPS advantage is especially large (+6.33 pts), demonstrating PathEDU students ramp to strong performance quickly.
  3. Education at Work is the #1 PS partner across all partners. Best New Hire AHT (17.59) and strong New Hire tNPS (47.52) — no other partner matches both dimensions.
  4. EAW Wave 6 is the standout wave (AHT 16.96, tNPS 57.60). Wave 7 underperforms (AHT 20.48) but has very few experts (16) and handles (1,665).
  5. Foundever PS shows mixed results by channel. PathEDU Foundever (19.26 AHT, 47.63 tNPS for New Hires) is competitive. NonPathEDU Foundever has the fastest AHT (17.27) but the lowest tNPS (36.69) among all partners.
  6. LiveOps and Working Solutions have the highest AHT (23+ and 21+ min respectively) among all partners, suggesting potential efficiency improvements there.

Agent Status Findings

  1. TermAfterPeak is the fairest comparison lens. When comparing only experts who completed the full season, PathEDU's advantage grows: PS New Hires are 1.8 min faster and 7.3 pts higher tNPS than NonPathEDU.
  2. Early attrition (TermBeforePeak) correlates with poor customer outcomes across both PathEDU and NonPathEDU. NonPathEDU PS TermBeforePeak tNPS is low (19.03), while PathEDU's is also weak (34.84) but notably better.
  3. No PathEDU experts remain Active — the program is fully seasonal. NonPathEDU retains hundreds of Active experts, whose strong metrics reflect self-selection (longest-tenure, best performers).

University & Academic Standing Findings

  1. Arizona State University and UT El Paso are the volume universities — together accounting for the majority of PathEDU experts — and both deliver solid results.
  2. University of Utah stands out on PS (best AHT 16.07, strong tNPS 53.96) and on TS (strong tNPS 84.72). Small cohort but consistent excellence.
  3. Northern Kentucky University underperforms on customer satisfaction in both PS (tNPS 23.12) and TS (tNPS 33.33). May warrant program review or targeted support.
  4. Freshmen and Juniors deliver the best PS tNPS (~51) while Freshmen lead on TS tNPS (87.88) — younger students aren't at a disadvantage and may bring more enthusiasm.
  5. Seniors are the most efficient across both roles (lowest CST/AHT) and have the highest TS conversion rate (61.55%), but lower tNPS — a speed-vs-satisfaction trade-off.

Strategic Implications

  1. PathEDU is a high-value pipeline. Across both roles, PathEDU students deliver comparable or superior performance, especially on customer satisfaction metrics. The TermAfterPeak lens strengthens this conclusion.
  2. Invest in efficiency and conversion training for TS. PathEDU's elevated CST and AHT, combined with lower conversion, suggest students need coaching on call efficiency and sales assertiveness. The SKU mix analysis confirms this is a behavioral gap, not a complexity issue — PathEDU handles simpler cases yet takes longer. The strong tNPS shows they connect well with customers — the goal is to maintain satisfaction while reducing handle time.
  3. Expand Education at Work partnership. EAW consistently delivers the strongest metrics across both TS and PS. The partnership is well-calibrated and scalable.
  4. Monitor Foundever's TS program carefully. Very small cohorts make metrics unreliable. Foundever's PathEDU PS program is more established and shows stronger signal. Foundever's NonPathEDU PS workstream has fast AHT but lower tNPS — targeted coaching may help.
  5. Address Wave 7 efficiency dip in PS. Both EAW and Foundever Wave 7 PS cohorts show elevated AHT — this may reflect a recent wave still ramping up.
  6. Investigate Northern Kentucky University performance. Consistently low tNPS in both PS and TS suggests a training or support gap at this school.
  7. Reduce early attrition. TermBeforePeak experts deliver poor customer outcomes regardless of channel — retention efforts during ramp-up could improve both metrics and ROI.
