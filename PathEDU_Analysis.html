2. Program Context

  • PathEDU is an Intuit program that trains and hires university students to handle customer calls — a different pipeline from the traditional "street to seat" hiring channel.
  • Two roles: Tax Specialists (TS) handle tax-related customer inquiries; Product Support (PS) handles product/technical issues.
  • Two partners participating this year:
    • Education at Work — exclusively works with PathEDU (both TS and PS)
    • Foundever — only Marshall University participates in PathEDU; Foundever also has many other non-PathEDU workstreams
  • NonPathEDU comparison: For TS, the comparison is against Intuit-sourced experts. For PS, NonPathEDU includes multiple partners (Teleperformance, Concentrix, LiveOps, Working Solutions, and Foundever's non-PathEDU workstreams).

6. Conclusions & Key Takeaways

Tax Specialist Findings

  1. PathEDU delivers higher customer satisfaction. FS tNPS is 3.70 points higher (83.24 vs 79.54) overall. Re-Hires achieve strong tNPS (87.07).
  2. PathEDU delivers lower CST (2.18 vs 2.78 — 21.58% more efficient). Per-SKU CST analysis confirms PathEDU outperforms within every product category (Basic: 1.84 vs 2.03, Deluxe: 2.42 vs 2.83, Premium: 3.10 vs 4.13). AHT remains elevated (27.70 vs 25.57 min).
  3. Conversion rate needs attention. PathEDU lags by 2.53 pp overall (60.54% vs 63.07%), with Re-Hires showing a wider gap (−5.06 pp). Targeted conversion training could close this gap.
  4. Education at Work is the strong TS partner. Consistent 82+ tNPS for New Hires, 87.07 for Re-Hires, and reasonable volume. Foundever's TS program is too small for reliable conclusions.
  5. EAW Wave 3 has the best balance (tNPS 83.81, Conv 62.24%, lowest CST 2.07). Wave 4 shows slightly higher CST (2.37) with peak tNPS (83.91); Wave 5 trails on satisfaction — sustain quality and efficiency in later cohorts.

Product Support Findings

  1. PathEDU outperforms on both key metrics. AHT is 10.47% faster (17.53 vs 19.58 min) and tNPS is 2.98 points higher (48.04 vs 45.06). This is a strong result — faster and better-rated.
  2. The advantage holds for both New Hires and Re-Hires. New Hire tNPS advantage is especially large (+6.32 pts), demonstrating PathEDU students ramp to strong performance quickly.
  3. Education at Work is the #1 PS PathEDU partner. Strong New Hire AHT (17.59) and New Hire tNPS (47.52) versus NonPathEDU peers.
  4. EAW Wave 6 leads on tNPS (54.35) with efficient AHT (17.16). Wave 7 shows higher AHT (20.87) with solid tNPS (51.08) on 38 experts.
  5. Foundever NonPathEDU PS has the fastest New Hire AHT among NonPathEDU partners (17.35) but the lowest New Hire tNPS in that group (37.37).
  6. LiveOps and Working Solutions have the highest AHT (22.23 and 20.03 min respectively) among all partners, suggesting potential efficiency improvements there.

Agent Status Findings

  1. TermAfterPeak is the fairest comparison lens. When comparing only experts who completed the full season, PathEDU's advantage grows: PS New Hires are 1.76 min faster and 7.34 pts higher tNPS than NonPathEDU.
  2. Early attrition (TermBeforePeak) correlates with poor customer outcomes across both PathEDU and NonPathEDU. NonPathEDU PS TermBeforePeak tNPS is low (19.20), while PathEDU's is also weak (34.50) but notably better.
  3. No PathEDU experts remain Active — the program is fully seasonal. NonPathEDU retains hundreds of Active experts, whose strong metrics reflect self-selection (longest-tenure, best performers).

University & Academic Standing Findings

  1. Arizona State University and UT El Paso are the volume universities — together accounting for the majority of PathEDU experts — and both deliver solid results.
  2. University of Utah stands out on PS (best AHT 16.03, strong tNPS 54.25) and on TS (strong CST 2.01, tNPS 85.19). Small cohort but consistent excellence.
  3. Northern Kentucky University underperforms on customer satisfaction in both PS (tNPS 21.18) and TS (tNPS 33.33). May warrant program review or targeted support.
  4. Juniors deliver the best PS tNPS (51.39) while Freshmen lead on TS tNPS (88.00) — younger students aren't at a disadvantage and may bring more enthusiasm.
  5. Seniors are highly efficient across both roles (strong CST 2.21/AHT 17.44) and have a high TS conversion rate (60.92%), with strong tNPS (82.39) in TS.

Strategic Implications

  1. PathEDU is a high-value pipeline. Across both roles, PathEDU students deliver comparable or superior performance, especially on customer satisfaction metrics. The TermAfterPeak lens strengthens this conclusion.
  2. Leverage PathEDU's CST advantage. PathEDU experts are 21.58% more efficient on CST (2.18 vs 2.78) and outperform within every SKU. The remaining AHT gap (27.70 vs 25.57 min) and conversion deficit (60.54% vs 63.07%) are the primary coaching opportunities. Focus conversion coaching on Basic returns where PathEDU's heavier mix may contribute to the gap.
  3. Expand Education at Work partnership. EAW consistently delivers the strongest metrics across both TS and PS. The partnership is well-calibrated and scalable.
  4. Monitor Foundever's TS program carefully. Very small cohorts make metrics unreliable. Foundever's PathEDU PS program is more established and shows stronger signal. Foundever's NonPathEDU PS workstream has fast AHT but lower tNPS — targeted coaching may help.
  5. Address Wave 7 efficiency dip in PS. Both EAW and Foundever Wave 7 PS cohorts show elevated AHT — this may reflect a recent wave still ramping up.
  6. Investigate Northern Kentucky University performance. Consistently low tNPS in both PS and TS suggests a training or support gap at this school.
  7. Reduce early attrition. TermBeforePeak experts deliver poor customer outcomes regardless of channel — retention efforts during ramp-up could improve both metrics and ROI.
