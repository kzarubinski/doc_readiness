1. Executive Summary

This analysis examines how performance differs across hiring waves — comparing earlier waves to later ones for both New Hires and Re-Hires across three partners (Highspring, JDA, Magnit). Five core metrics are evaluated: FS CST, FS tNPS, TTLA AHT, TTLA tNPS, and Conversion Rate.

2. Methodology, Metric Definitions & Wave Date Ranges

Metric Definitions

  • FS CST — Full Service Customer Service Time (lower is better). Computed as fs_cst_num / fs_eng.
  • FS tNPS — Full Service transactional Net Promoter Score (higher is better). Computed as fs_tnps_num / fs_tnps_denom × 100.
  • TTLA AHT — TurboTax Live Assist Average Handle Time in minutes (lower is better). Computed as ttla_handle_min / ttla_handle_cnt.
  • TTLA tNPS — TurboTax Live Assist transactional NPS (higher is better). Computed as ttla_tnps_num / ttla_tnps_denom × 100.
  • Conversion Rate — Rate at which an expert turns an engagement into a completion (higher is better). Computed as conv_num / conv_denom × 100.
  • Aggregation: When combining rows (e.g., across partners or role types), numerators and denominators are summed before computing the ratio — yielding volume-weighted metrics.
  • Low-volume flag: Rows with fewer than 10 experts or negligible engagement volumes are displayed in muted italic and excluded from trend conclusions.

Key Findings

  1. Later New Hire waves consistently underperform earlier waves. This pattern holds across all three partners, both role types, and virtually every metric. The decline is not a one-partner anomaly — it is systemic.
  2. FS tNPS is the most impacted metric, dropping 18–22 points from peak (Wave 2) to Wave 6 across both Associates and Experts. This indicates later-wave hires are delivering notably worse customer experiences.
  3. TTLA tNPS follows the same trajectory, declining ~19 points for Associates (68.0% → 49.0%) and ~9 points for Experts (69.8% → 60.9%). Customer satisfaction is eroding across both service channels.
  4. TTLA AHT increased modestly overall (+3.6 min for NH Associates), but the impact is dramatically uneven by partner: JDA remained relatively stable (+2.6 min), while Highspring (+12.2 min W4→W6) and Magnit (+6.8 min W4→W5) saw large spikes.
  5. Conversion Rate declined ~3 points overall for NH Associates (73.3% → 70.5%), but the partner-level picture is more nuanced. The decline is steepest at Magnit (64.6% → 41.3% by W6) and moderate at Highspring (66.2% → 59.8%), while JDA held steady (73.3% → 72.8%).
  6. Magnit shows the most severe quality degradation across later waves, with the highest AHT levels, sharpest tNPS drops, and steepest conversion decline. This suggests a particular challenge with later-wave onboarding quality or candidate pool.
  7. Re-Hire Wave 1 (JDA) is the gold standard. With 687+ Associates and 160 Experts, this cohort outperforms almost all New Hire waves, confirming that experienced re-hires deliver superior results. Later RH waves are too small for reliable conclusions.
  8. FS CST is less impacted than other metrics. While it worsened slightly (2.82 → 3.02 for NH Associates), the magnitude is smaller than tNPS or conversion declines, suggesting later-wave hires maintain reasonable service speed but struggle with quality outcomes.
Bottom Line: Later hiring waves (particularly Waves 5 and 6) are producing experts who perform measurably worse than their earlier-wave counterparts across customer satisfaction (tNPS), service quality (CST), handle time (AHT), and conversion rate. The decline is most severe at Magnit and Highspring, while JDA shows a more moderate degradation in satisfaction scores with stable conversion. This signals potential issues with later-wave training effectiveness, candidate quality, or insufficient ramp time. JDA's large RH Wave 1 cohort demonstrates that experienced re-hires consistently outperform new hires, suggesting retention and re-hire strategies should be prioritized.
