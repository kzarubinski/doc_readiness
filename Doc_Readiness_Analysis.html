1. Executive Summary

The Doc Readiness initiative requires that ≥80% of documentation is uploaded before an engagement progresses to the "Prep Return" stage. The hypothesis: having most documents available upfront would reduce overall Customer Service Time (CST). While overall CST did not improve for the test group — and in fact increased modestly — several meaningful wins emerged.

Bottom Line: Overall CST increased in the test period, meaning the initiative did not achieve its primary goal of reducing CST. However, the initiative succeeded in shifting more engagements into the doc-ready (≥80%) bucket (+10.4 pp), and the CST advantage of doc-ready engagements over non-ready ones widened significantly — especially when same-day completions are excluded. 2. Methodology & Initiative Overview

Initiative Design

  • Goal: Require ≥80% of documentation to be uploaded before progressing to "Prep Return" stage
  • Hypothesis: Having documentation available upfront reduces CST by eliminating wait times for missing documents
  • Control Period (Pre-Rollout): 2/1/2026 – 3/8/2026
  • Test Period (Post-Rollout): 3/9/2026 – 4/15/2026
  • Metric: CST (Customer Service Time) — lower is better
  • Segmentation: Engagements split into ≥80% Docs Ready and <80% Docs Ready
  • Channels: Internal (Intuit) and External
  • Additional cut: Same-day completed returns excluded to isolate non-trivial engagements

8. Key Wins & Positive Signals

Summary of Wins

1. Doc-Ready Engagement Rate Increased by +10.4 pp

The initiative's primary behavioral goal — getting more documentation uploaded upfront — was achieved. The doc-ready rate rose from 54.0% to 64.4% overall, with External gaining +12.3 pp and Internal +10.0 pp. This demonstrates that the "Prep Return" gate is influencing upstream behavior.

2. CST Gap Between ≥80% and <80% Groups Widened by 56%

Doc-ready engagements showed a 0.28 CST advantage over non-ready ones in the test period (vs. 0.18 in control). This means the initiative is amplifying the CST benefit of doc readiness — engagements that meet the threshold are being processed meaningfully faster relative to those that don't.

3. Excluding Same-Day: CST Gap Nearly Doubled (+83%)

For non-trivial engagements (same-day completions excluded), the CST gap jumped from −0.24 (Control) to −0.44 (Test) — an 83% increase. This is the strongest evidence that doc readiness delivers real value where it matters most: complex, multi-step returns.

4. Internal (Intuit) CST Gap More Than Doubled

Internal engagements saw the gap grow from −0.15 to −0.33 (full data) and from −0.19 to −0.44 (excl. same-day). The 2.3× improvement in the same-day-excluded view is a strong validation of the initiative's mechanism for internal workflows.

5. <80% Group CST Increased More Than ≥80% Group

While both groups saw CST increases (likely seasonal), the <80% group's CST rose more sharply (+0.44 overall) compared to the ≥80% group (+0.34). This suggests that doc-ready engagements are more resilient to seasonal/systemic CST pressures — they maintained relatively better performance during a period of overall CST inflation.

6. Non-Trivial Engagement Doc-Ready Rate Jumped +11.3 pp

When same-day completions are excluded, the doc-ready rate shifted from 51.3% to 62.5% — a +11.3 pp gain. Notably, the control period had a near-equal split (51.3% vs. 48.7%), while the test period pushed the majority of non-trivial engagements into the doc-ready bucket. This is especially meaningful because these are the engagements where doc readiness has the most room to impact CST.

9. Conclusions & Recommendations

Key Findings

  1. The initiative did not reduce overall CST — blended CST increased in the test period. However, this appears to be driven by external/seasonal factors rather than the initiative itself, as both doc-ready and non-ready groups saw CST increases.
  2. Behavioral change was successful. The doc-ready rate increased by +10.4 pp overall, proving the "Prep Return" gate effectively drives earlier document upload. External customers responded most strongly (+12.3 pp).
  3. The mechanism works: Doc-ready engagements consistently outperform non-ready ones on CST, and this advantage grew during the test period — by 56% in full data and 83% when same-day completions are excluded.
  4. Non-trivial engagements benefit most. When same-day completions are removed, the CST gap nearly doubles (−0.24 → −0.44), confirming that doc readiness has its greatest impact on complex returns.
  5. Doc-ready engagements are more resilient to CST inflation. During a period of rising CST, doc-ready engagements saw smaller increases than non-ready ones, suggesting upfront documentation acts as a buffer against processing delays.
  6. Internal (Intuit) channel shows the strongest relative improvement, with the CST gap more than doubling in both full and same-day-excluded views.

Recommendations

  1. Continue the initiative. Despite overall CST not improving, the behavioral shift and widening CST gap provide strong evidence the mechanism works. The benefits may become more visible as the initiative matures and seasonal CST pressures subside.
  2. Target the 80% threshold more aggressively. The current 64.4% doc-ready rate leaves significant room for improvement. Pushing toward the original goal of >80% of engagements meeting the threshold could yield compounding CST savings.
  3. Isolate seasonal effects. Re-evaluate the initiative once seasonal CST pressures normalize, or compare year-over-year data for the same timeframes to better isolate the initiative's true impact.
  4. Focus on non-trivial engagements. The strongest signal appears when same-day completions are excluded. Consider tailoring messaging and enforcement for non-trivial engagements, where doc readiness delivers the greatest value.
  5. Investigate external channel gap narrowing. While the external doc-ready rate improved the most (+12.3 pp), the full-data CST gap narrowed. Determine whether this is driven by same-day return composition or other factors.
  6. Track "Prep Return" stage timing. Measure how quickly engagements progress from doc upload to Prep Return — a reduction in stage transition time would provide additional evidence of the initiative's process efficiency gains.
